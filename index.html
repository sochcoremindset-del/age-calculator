How to Calculate Chronological Age Manually

To compute chronological age by hand, align the test date on top of the birth date, writing both dates backward in Year-Month-Day order. Always subtract from right to left (days first, then months, then years), using borrowing rules when needed:

  • Borrowing Days: If the test day is smaller than the birth day, borrow 1 month from the test month. Convert that borrowed month into the exact number of calendar days of the previous month, add them to your test days, and reduce the test month count by 1.
  • Borrowing Months: If the test month is smaller than the birth month, borrow 1 year (12 months) from the test year, add them to the test month count, and reduce the test year count by 1.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the 15-day rounding rule for speech therapy assessments?

In many speech-language pathology tests, if a child's exact age includes 15 or more days, the age is rounded up to the next full month. If it includes 14 or fewer days, the days are discarded and the age is rounded down. This ensures accurate normative comparisons.
